DataSentinel Internal Policy — Data SLA and Quality Standards
Version 2.1 | Effective January 2024

PIPELINE FRESHNESS STANDARDS
All production payment pipelines must deliver data within 4 hours of 
the source timestamp. Any gap exceeding 6 hours is classified as a 
P1 incident requiring immediate escalation to the data engineering 
lead and VP of Engineering.

Freshness is measured as the difference between the latest record 
timestamp in the transformed layer and the current system time. 
Dashboards displaying data older than 6 hours must show a visible 
staleness warning to all viewers.

DATA VOLUME STANDARDS
Daily transaction volume is expected to remain within 20% of the 
30-day rolling average. A deviation greater than 20% downward triggers 
a P2 alert. A deviation greater than 40% downward triggers a P1 alert 
requiring immediate investigation.

Silent drops, defined as cases where rows_processed is less than 95% 
of rows_ingested in the pipeline run log, must be investigated and 
resolved within 2 business hours. Any silent drop exceeding 10% of 
daily volume must be reported to the Head of Data.

NULL RATE STANDARDS
No production column classified as NOT NULL in the data contract may 
exceed a 1% null rate in the transformed layer. The customer_dest 
field is classified as NOT NULL per the payment data contract v3.2. 
Any null rate above 2% in this field triggers an automatic P1 schema 
drift incident.

DUPLICATE RECORD STANDARDS
The acceptable duplicate rate for payment transactions is below 0.1%. 
Any duplicate rate above 0.5% must be investigated. Duplicates above 
1% require a full pipeline audit and rollback assessment.

VALUE RANGE STANDARDS
Transaction amounts must fall within statistically expected bounds. 
Any transaction amount exceeding 100 times the 95th percentile of 
the prior 30-day distribution is flagged for manual review. Amounts 
exceeding 1000 times the 95th percentile are classified as data 
corruption and the batch must be quarantined.
